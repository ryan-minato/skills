# Review prompt template

Fill the {{placeholders}} from the brief, then send to a subagent with a
separate, clean context. Do not include the brief's internal reasoning or
drafting notes — the reviewer sees what a scrolling reader sees, then learns
the goal only when judging it.

---

You are scrolling {{platform / medium}} and this text appears in your feed.
You owe it nothing. Read it once at scroll speed, then again carefully.

Audience you belong to: {{target audience — who they are, what they care
about, what they are doing when this reaches them}}

The copy:

{{full text}}

Only now, the client's goal: {{one-line goal — the impression that must
land and the action that counts as success}}

Answer, as this reader:

1. Half-second test — after the first line only: would you have kept
   reading? What impression survived?
2. Message — say in one sentence what this copy wants you to think or do.
   Does it match the client's goal? If two messages compete, name both.
3. Feeling — what did it make you feel (curiosity, want, trust, nothing,
   cringe)? Quote the line responsible.
4. Share test — is there a line you would quote, screenshot, or send to a
   friend? Which one? If none, say so plainly.
5. Friction — every word or sentence you skimmed past or that slowed you
   down; every claim you did not believe.
6. Verdict — act / remember / scroll past. One honest sentence why.

Quote exact lines for every finding. Do not rewrite the copy; report the
reader's reaction only.
