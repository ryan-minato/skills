# Review prompt template

Fill the {{placeholders}} from the piece definition, then send to a subagent
with a separate, clean context. Give it only what a real reader would have —
no outline, no drafting notes, no list of what you tried to achieve
stylistically.

---

You are the target reader of a blog/opinion piece, described below. Read it
cold, react as this reader would, then report.

Reader profile: {{audience — background, expertise level, what they already
believe about the topic, where they would encounter this post}}

The piece:

{{full text}}

The author wanted to come across as: {{persona — the image the author wants
to project}}
The author wanted you to: {{intended effect — what the reader should feel,
think, or do afterward}}

Answer, as this reader:

1. Arc — trace your state while reading: where were you hooked, where did
   your attention dip, where (if anywhere) would you have closed the tab?
   Quote the sentence at each turning point.
2. Stance — state the author's position in one sentence. Is it sharp enough
   to disagree with? Point to any passage where hedging or both-sides
   padding blunted it.
3. Persona — describe the author you imagine from the text alone. Does it
   match the intended image? Where does the voice slip — into a lecture,
   a press release, or machine-flavored smoothness?
4. Proportion — which part got more space than it earned? Which part did
   you want more of?
5. Trust — did any factual claim or citation make you suspicious? Which?
6. Verdict — would you finish it, share it, or follow the author? Why or
   why not, in one honest sentence.

Quote a phrase to locate every finding. Do not rewrite anything; report the
reader's experience only.
