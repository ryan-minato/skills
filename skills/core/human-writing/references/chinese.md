# Chinese — AI tells and repairs

Load this when drafting or reviewing a Chinese-language piece. Judge by
accumulation, not single occurrences.

## Vocabulary and phrasing tells

- Empty grand words: 赋能、抓手、闭环、颠覆性、里程碑式、深刻变革、
  不可或缺、举足轻重、毋庸置疑、值得注意的是. Repair: state the concrete
  fact that earned the adjective, or delete it.
- Translationese verb padding: “进行” + noun (“进行优化” → “优化”),
  “作出” + noun (“作出改进” → “改进”), “实现了...的提升” → “提升了...”.
- Overused passive “被” where Chinese would use active or topic-comment
  order: “这个问题被我们解决了” → “这个问题我们解决了” / “我们解决了这个
  问题”.
- Mechanical connectives opening every paragraph: 此外、然而、同时、
  总而言之、综上所述. Rely on sentence order; keep a connective only at a
  real logical turn.
- “不仅...更...” / “不仅仅是...而是...” negative parallelism as a default
  emphasis device. One per piece at most.
- Defensive endings: “当然，这因人而异”、“仅供参考”. Cut unless the author
  genuinely wants the caveat.

## Sentence-shape tells (欧化长句)

- Stacked pre-modifiers with chained 的: “一个基于云原生架构的、支持弹性
  扩容的、面向企业级用户的平台”. Repair: split into short clauses — “这个
  平台基于云原生架构，支持弹性扩容，面向企业级用户”.
- Clause-final “之一” everywhere (“最重要的因素之一”): commit or drop.
- Long subject + short predicate sentences translated from English syntax.
  Chinese reads better topic-first with short verb-final clauses.
- 的的不休: three or more 的 in one clause almost always splits into two
  clauses cleanly.

## Punctuation and format tells

- Mixing full-width and half-width punctuation; Chinese prose uses full-width
  （，。：；？！）, with half-width reserved for inline code and numbers.
- 破折号（——）and colon overuse for dramatic pauses; prefer 逗号 or a new
  sentence.
- Curly vs straight quotes inconsistency; pick “” for prose and stay with it.
- Bullet lists with bolded lead-ins substituting for paragraphs; write
  prose unless items are genuinely parallel.

## Register repairs

- Replace degree adverbs (非常、极其、十分) with numbers or observable
  facts.
- 成语 add register but stack badly: at most one per passage, and only when
  exact.
- Prefer short sentences and verb-driven clauses; vary length for rhythm —
  uniform 20-character sentences read as generated.
