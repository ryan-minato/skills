# English — AI tells and repairs

Load this when drafting or reviewing an English-language piece. Patterns are
signals, not verdicts: flag a passage when several stack up, not because one
word appears once.

## Vocabulary and phrasing tells

- AI-flavored word cluster: delve, underscore, pivotal, crucial, robust,
  seamless, landscape, tapestry, realm, testament, meticulous, intricate,
  showcase, foster, leverage, vibrant, boast, garner, elevate, holistic.
  Any of these can be legitimate; a paragraph carrying three or four reads
  as generated.
- Copula avoidance: "serves as", "stands as", "functions as", "represents",
  "acts as" where "is" would do. Repair: use "is/are/has" and spend the saved
  words on a concrete detail.
- Hedging stacks: "it's important to note that", "arguably", "can
  potentially", "in many ways". Repair: delete the hedge or replace it with
  the actual condition ("on ARM builds, this fails").
- Empty intensifiers and evaluation: "truly", "incredibly", "a testament to",
  "plays a vital role in". Repair: show the fact that justifies the
  evaluation and cut the adjective.
- Formulaic transitions opening every paragraph: Moreover, Furthermore,
  Additionally, Overall, In conclusion. Repair: order ideas so they connect
  by content; keep an explicit connective only where the logic genuinely
  turns (But, So, Instead).
- False ranges: "from X to Y" spanning items that are not on a scale
  ("from data pipelines to team culture"). Repair: name the items plainly.

## Structure and rhythm tells

- Negative parallelism as default: "not just X, but Y", "it's not about X,
  it's about Y". One per piece is rhetoric; one per section is a tic.
- Rule-of-three chains: "fast, simple, and reliable" triads recurring in
  every paragraph. Vary list length; two items or four are allowed.
- Uniform paragraph length and shape — every paragraph three sentences, every
  section one paragraph plus one list. Human rhythm varies with content.
- Synonym rotation to avoid repeating a term ("the tool → the utility → the
  solution → the platform"). Repeating the right word is correct technical
  style; rotate only when the repetition is genuinely clumsy.
- Summary endings that re-list what the reader just read ("In conclusion,
  we have seen..."). End on the point with the most force instead: the
  consequence, the open question, the call to act.

## Register repairs

- Prefer active voice and a named actor; passive is fine when the actor is
  unknown or irrelevant.
- Contractions (it's, don't) are correct in any register short of formal
  print; their total absence is itself a tell.
- Sentence-initial And/But/So are legitimate in essays and blogs.
- Concrete beats abstract: "cut startup from 8 s to 1.2 s" over "significantly
  improved performance".
